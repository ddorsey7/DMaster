The Kitten in the Box

  • High Concept: Your a cat in a box, avoiding killer balls.
  • Player Experience Goals: The experience I want the player to feel is fun and stress at the same time.
  • Genre: A casual game, like candy crush, with little strategy and more timing.
  • Game play: The player will dodge the bouncing balls and try to survive for 60 seconds. The player has a life of 1000 points. Each ball hit is -20 points. If the cat get hit hundred times then the player dies. The best strategy is too not the balls for they will bounce faster and off each other.
  • Prototype goal: The game mechanic this is prototype evaluating the bouncing physics and player's reaction time.
  • Features: The unique aspects of my game that set it apart is it all about dodging and nothing else. It's simplistic and fun.
  • Setting/Premise: Your a cat trapped in a box, avoiding killer bouncing balls for a whole minute.
  • Story: If the game has a story, summarize it in a paragraph. How will the dramatic tension interact with the gameplay tension?
  • Target Audience: People have nothing else to do. Mostly likely children from ages 3-9.
  • Play time: One minute
  • Strategies: Player strategies that will be effective at playing this game would be not knocking any of balls and move slowly.
  • Prototype Assets: Assets use where a cat drawing, ball animation, and a desposit photo of a box.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Games that most closely resembles my game are "Bounce and Knock" and "Group vs. Self."
